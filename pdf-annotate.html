What is PDF Annotation?

PDF annotation is the process of adding notes, comments, highlights, drawings, and other markup to PDF documents. Annotations allow you to mark up documents for review, collaborate with team members, study more effectively, and provide feedback without altering the original content. Our PDF annotation tool provides a complete set of markup tools including highlights, underlines, sticky notes, text boxes, freehand drawing, shapes, and arrows, all processed locally in your browser for complete privacy.

Understanding Annotation Types

Highlight

Highlight text to draw attention to important passages. Perfect for studying, reviewing, and marking key information. Highlights are semi-transparent and appear behind text. Choose from various colors (yellow is traditional, but any color works).

Underline

Add underlines beneath text to emphasize important content. Great for marking definitions, key terms, or passages that need attention. Underlines are crisp and professional.

Sticky Note

Add virtual sticky notes anywhere on the page for comments, questions, or reminders. Click to place a note icon, then type your note text. Perfect for leaving feedback or making personal notes.

Add Text

Insert text boxes anywhere on the page to add comments, labels, or additional information. Great for filling out forms, adding instructions, or providing context.

Draw (Freehand)

Freehand drawing tool for creating custom shapes, circling items, or adding signatures. Use with different colors and brush sizes for maximum flexibility.

Rectangle

Draw rectangle shapes to box in important content, create borders, or highlight specific areas. Perfect for emphasizing charts, images, or sections of text.

Circle

Circle important elements in your PDF - ideal for drawing attention to specific points, circling answers, or highlighting circular areas.

Arrow

Draw arrow shapes to point to specific content, indicate direction, or show relationships between elements. Excellent for callouts and explanations.

Technical Details: How Our Annotation Tool Works

Our PDF annotation tool uses a sophisticated multi-layer architecture:

  • PDF Rendering with PDF.js: PDF pages are rendered to canvas for display and interaction.
  • Canvas Overlay: Annotations are drawn on an overlay canvas above the PDF, allowing for real-time editing.
  • Annotation Storage: All annotations are stored in memory with position, type, color, size, and content data.
  • PDF Embedding with PDF-lib: When saving, annotations are embedded into the PDF as proper PDF annotations using PDF-lib's drawing functions.
  • Coordinate Mapping: Screen coordinates are converted to PDF coordinates (points) for accurate placement.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Annotations Not Saving?

Ensure you click "Save Annotations to PDF" before closing the page. The save button embeds annotations into the PDF. Always download the saved file from the result section.

Drawing Not Appearing?

Check that you're using the correct tool and that the color contrasts with the page background. Dark colors on dark backgrounds may be invisible. Adjust color and try again.

Can't Select Text to Highlight?

Our highlight tool works by clicking and dragging over text. PDF.js renders PDFs as images, so highlighting is area-based rather than text-selection based. Drag a rectangle over the text you want to highlight.

Annotations on Wrong Page?

Always check the page number before adding annotations. Use the page navigation to ensure you're on the correct page. Annotations are page-specific.
