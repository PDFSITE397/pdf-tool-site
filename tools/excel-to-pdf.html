First Sheet Only: Converts only the first worksheet in your Excel workbook. Use this when your Excel file has a summary or dashboard sheet as the first sheet, or when you only need the primary data sheet from a multi-sheet workbook.

Active/Selected Sheet: Allows you to choose which specific sheet to convert. After uploading, the tool displays all sheet names, and you can select the one you need. Perfect for large workbooks where you only need one specific data table.

Page Orientation

Portrait: Pages are taller than they are wide. Best for spreadsheets with fewer columns (typically 6-8 columns) and many rows. Ideal for lists, inventories, and data with many records but limited fields.

Landscape (Recommended): Pages are wider than they are tall. Best for most business spreadsheets that have many columns (10+ columns) including financial statements, sales reports, and data analyses. Landscape orientation prevents column wrapping and maintains readability.

Paper Sizes

Letter (8.5" x 11"): Standard paper size in North America. Best for US-based documents and printing on standard office printers.

Legal (8.5" x 14"): Longer than Letter, good for spreadsheets with more rows while maintaining width.

A4 (210mm x 297mm): International standard paper size, used in most countries outside North America. Our recommended setting for global document sharing.

Tabloid (11" x 17"): Large format for wide spreadsheets with many columns, ideal for complex financial models and data dashboards.

Technical Details: How Our Excel to PDF Converter Works

Our Excel to PDF converter uses a sophisticated two-stage conversion process:

  • Excel Parsing with SheetJS: The Excel file is parsed to extract all data including values, formatting hints, sheet names, and metadata. SheetJS reads both modern XLSX and legacy XLS formats, preserving numbers, text, dates, and formulas (as calculated values).
  • Data Extraction: For each selected sheet, the converter extracts the used range (cells containing data), determines column widths based on content, and identifies header rows.
  • PDF Generation with PDF-lib: A new PDF document is created with pages sized according to your selected paper size and orientation. Data is rendered as text with proper positioning, fonts, and alignment.
  • Table Formatting: The converter creates a table structure in the PDF with proper column spacing, row heights, and borders (if gridlines are enabled).
  • Multi-Page Handling: If the table exceeds one page, content is automatically split across multiple pages with headers repeated on each page for context.

Best Practices for Excel to PDF Conversion

Prepare Your Excel File First

For best results, prepare your Excel spreadsheet before conversion:

  • Set print areas to define exactly what should be converted
  • Adjust column widths to ensure all content fits
  • Use clear headers for each column
  • Remove unnecessary hidden rows or columns
  • Check that all data is visible (not cut off by cell formatting)

Choose the Right Orientation

Select landscape orientation for spreadsheets with more than 8 columns. Landscape prevents columns from wrapping to the next line, maintaining readability. For narrow tables with few columns, portrait orientation works well and saves paper when printing.

Use Fit to Page Width

Always enable "Fit to page width" unless you have specific sizing requirements. This ensures your entire spreadsheet width fits on one page horizontally, preventing cutoff columns. The content will scale appropriately while remaining readable.

Test with a Sample First

If converting an important document, test the conversion with a sample or a single sheet first to verify that formatting, column widths, and data alignment meet your expectations. Adjust Excel formatting if needed and reconvert.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Table Cut Off on Right Side?

If your table extends beyond the page width, try these solutions:

  • Enable "Fit to page width" option
  • Switch to landscape orientation
  • Select a larger paper size (Tabloid or Legal)
  • Reduce column widths in Excel before converting
  • Hide unnecessary columns in Excel

Fonts Look Different in PDF?

Our converter uses standard PDF fonts (Helvetica, Times, Courier) for maximum compatibility. If your Excel uses custom fonts, the PDF will substitute with the closest standard font. For consistent appearance, use standard fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman in your Excel file.

Charts and Images Missing?

Our current version focuses on tabular data extraction. Complex charts and embedded images may not convert perfectly. For documents with many charts, consider using our "Print to PDF" feature from Excel directly. We're continuously improving chart support for future updates.

Dates Showing as Numbers?

Excel stores dates as serial numbers. Our converter attempts to detect and format dates properly, but some date formats may appear as numbers. Review converted PDF and adjust date formatting in Excel before re-converting if needed.

Large Files Taking Too Long?

Very large Excel files (50+ MB or 10,000+ rows) may take a moment to process. This is normal as the converter parses all data. For better performance, consider splitting the Excel file into smaller workbooks or converting only specific sheets.

Understanding Excel Limitations in PDF

When converting Excel to PDF, understand these limitations:

  • Formulas: PDFs store calculated values, not the formulas themselves. Recipients see the results but cannot see how calculations were performed.
  • Conditional Formatting: Colors and formatting applied through conditional formatting are preserved as static formatting in the PDF.
  • Data Validation: Dropdown lists and validation rules are not functional in PDFs.
  • Macros: VBA code and macros do not transfer to PDF.
  • Hyperlinks: Basic hyperlinks to websites are preserved and clickable in the PDF.
